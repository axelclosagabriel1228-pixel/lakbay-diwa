Increasing Voters Awareness in the Philippines

Democracy thrives when its citizens understand their rights, responsibilities, and the power of their vote. Voter Awarness ensures citizens make informed decisions, resist manipulation, and strengthen the democratic foundation of the Philippines.

Why Voter's awareness matters?

  • A vote is a tool of change- Every ballot shapes national and local governance
  • Informed voting prevents corruption- Choosing leaders based on competence reduces corruption
  • Democracy strengthens with participation- Higher voter turnout reflects a healthy democracy and the true will of the people
  • Knowledgeable voters are harder to manipulate- Awareness reduces fake news and vote buying influence

Understanding the Filipino Electoral Process

To vote wisley citizens must understand how the electoral system works.

    National elections for President and Vice President occur every six years. Senators are elected every three years for a term of six years. Local Officals such as governors, mayors, and councilors are also elected every three years. Filipino citizens must be at least 18 years old on election day to vote and must be registered under COMELEC The National ID may be used as a valid ID for voter registration and identification at polling stations. Voting is done through secret ballot to ensure privacy and freedom of choice. Absentee voting is available for Filipinos abroad through the Overseas Absentee Voting program.

Crucial Issues Every Filipino Voter should understand

  • Vote-Buying - Illegal and damaging to democracy.
  • Political Dynasties - Evaluate whether they promote genuine service.
  • Fake News - Verify sources before sharing information.
  • Personality Politics - Prioritize platforms and integrity over popularity.
  • Sectoral Representation - Ensure candidates address the needs of marginalized groups.

How to choose the right candidate?

  • Track Record - Look for real accomplishments.
  • Education & Competence - Assess their preparedness to lead.
  • Character & Integrity - Avoid candidates linked to corruption.
  • Platform & Vision - Review plans for economy, education, and public welfare.
  • Leadership Style - Seek unifying and humane leadership.
  • Transparency - Favor candidates who openly share plans and finances.

Remember, an informed vote is a powerful vote. Your choice shapes the future of the Philippines.

How to Promote Voters' Awareness in the Community

  • Conduct barangay forums and youth discussions.
  • Organize mock elections in schools.
  • Promote fact-checking on social media.
  • Distribute flyers and educational materials.
  • Partner with groups like PPCRV and NAMFREL.

Voter Responsibilities

  • Register on time.
  • Vote wisely and conscientiously.
  • Avoid vote-buying.
  • Verify information from reliable sources.
  • Report election irregularities.
  • Monitor officials after elections.
